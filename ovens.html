Ovens

The first wood-burning oven ever built was in 1490 in Alsace, France. In 1728, the Germans introduced the five plate iron oven which was further developed in the early 1800s by Benjamin Thompson for use in big kitchens. The invention of the kerosene oven and the baseburner soon followed. The 20th century saw the invention of the gas-operated ovens and electrical ovens. The latest innovation is the microwave oven, invented by American physicist Percy Spencer.
